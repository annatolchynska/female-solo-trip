Solo travel for women is one of the best ways to learn about yourself and the world. Taking a solo trip is a fantastic way to grow. And when it comes to the best solo travel destinations, there are so many choices!

Lifehacks

  • Pick the right destination

    One of the most important thing as a solo female traveller is to choose your destination wisely. If this is your first time travelling alone, it’s ok to feel nervous about your adventure! The best thing to calm your nerves is to be comfortable with the destination you’re going to. It might not be the smartest thing to go on a solo exploring Amazon rainforest for your first solo trip.. Unless you’re sure about what you’re doing, stick with destinations that have proven safe for women travelling alone. Destinations like Bali, Europe and Australia are excellent for solo travellers.

  • Have at least your first two nights booked

    Nothing is better than having a place to stay when arriving and immediately getting a shower and not wasting time on searching a place to stay.

  • Do your research

    Make sure to google some things about the place you're going: cultural specifics, scams and danger etc.

  • Have an access to an unlimited internet

    It's nice to have peace of mind knowing you can always use your phone to make a call or use the GPS if you need to.

  • Documents and money

    Always keep your money (cash/credit card) in safe places from pickpocketing and have digital copies of your documents in case they're stolen

  • Pack light

    Surely, you don't want to be lugging around heavy bags when you have to get somewhere, especially when you don't have anyone to help you. Stick with the travel essentials and items that you really need.

  • Listen to your guts!

    When you feel like something doesn't feel right, you are most likely correct. So, follow your gut! If you don't feel safe in a situation, get out of there right away.
